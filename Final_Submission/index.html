Let's a take a trip back in time!

Between 1861 and 1865, the American Civil War was fought with shared blood and an unwritten bond between man and beast. It is said that one of the most valuable resources that afforded success in the American Civil War was the horse. Like they're human counterparts, the horse marched into war as a soldier, as a means of transportation, as a means of delivery, as a companion. Without their aid in cavalry, the length and outcome of the civil war would have been decidedly different. From the movement of infantry, to the transportation of weapons and artillery, the war lived and died on the backs of the steed.
